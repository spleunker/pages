Is Wayland Ready?

In short, mostly. The bare essentials are fully functional but what essentials? Things like: status bars, app-launchers, terminals etc. Everything you’d need for a window-manager, but there are still some minor annoyances here and there.

One being the cursor’s size or “style” switching between programs. It may take a few more months until all these minor problems have been ridden off but Wayland certainly does have the potentiality to replace Xorg. Look at GNOME! A complete desktop environment, if compatiable, completely running on Wayland, if that isn’t the case it’s probably running on Xwayland instead. But with nvidia open-sourcing their drivers this may change.

My recommendation would be this just play around a bit and dip your feet into the water. The first Wayland compositor I’d recommend would be sway, one of the more popular and easily configurable Wayland compositors. It’s much more similiar to i3 over from Xorg. But if’d you like something similiar to dwm; dwl might do it for you!
